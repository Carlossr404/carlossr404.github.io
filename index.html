About Me

Not-So-Short Story

I'm a computer nerd, born and raised in Nashville, TN. My interest with technology started with video games (original, ain't it?), particularly with taking apart my PlayStation 3 in order to expand storage. My initial success conducting "surgery" on my console encouraged my curiosity for tech, which turned into a passion that I've since integrated into all aspects of my life

In middle school, I developed a technical literacy course for Casa de la Cultura, a non-profit organization based in Nashville, TN. It wasn't until High School, however, that I was introduced to coding through the Scool for Science and Math at Vanderbilt (SSMV). Through this program, I was able to intern at the Learning in Virtual Environments (LiVE) Lab and conduct research in Augmented Reality alongside graduate students at Vandy. Here, I earned my first publications (which you can check out below) and learned a lot about working with others in a more professional setting.

After graduating High School, I continued conducting research at my now alma-matter: Rhodes College. This time, conducting perceptual research in Virtual Reality. There, I studied ways to treat phantom limb pain through VR and also worked to integrate our (new at the time) motion capture system with the VR lab's virtual environments. Once I obtained my Bachelor of Science in Computer Science, I went on to work in the healthcare industry, integrating AI technology into medical documentation

Currently, I do freelance work training Large Language Models, specifically evaluating code responses from various LLMs to ensure accuracy. I'm also currently enrolled in Cook System's Fast Track'd program to build my skills as a Fullstack Developer.
